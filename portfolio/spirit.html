A Spooky Inside Look at Spirit Halloween

October 9, 2020 - Matthew Novelli

Seasonal jobs are one thing, what about seasonal stores? Spirit Halloween is one of the only “seasonal retailers” in America. Owned by Spencer’s Gifts, Spirit Halloween (or just “Spirit”) has been taking over vacant retail storefronts since 1984 to sell Halloween-themed costumes and decorations.

I sat down with my friend Dillon Benson – Spirit’s newest employee – via Zoom to learn more about Spirit. This interview has been edited for length and clarity.

What do you do at Spirit?

My official title is “Spirit Halloween Sales Associate,” which is a generic term for underling. I do whatever the management needs me to do – I work the register and “walk the floor,” which is my favorite thing to do. Most of the time I help people “fix their costumes” by upselling them on a bunch of stuff.

The animatronics are really popular. A lot of people will come in asking if we have a certain animatronic to which I’ll say no. And no, we don’t have a “back room” that we can check.

How did you hear about this job?

My manager from the gym I used to work at is now a Spirit Halloween regional manager. When they started building the stores this year, she told me they needed “strong boys” like me. So, I helped build the store and now I work here.

What was the process of taking over a store like?

It’s a lot of fixing problems as they come up. The store I work at used to be a gym, so it has a nice big bathroom with locker rooms. We had to block off the men’s bathroom – to convert the women’s bathroom to unisex – so they had us take down the signs. That’s where I got this sign from [Dillon held up a men’s’ bathroom sign].

Tell me more about “building” the store.

It’s really simple I guess. The walls we put up are really heavy, big and finnicky, and I hate it. We have to wire the speakers around the back of the portable walls which sucks. We also have to put wooden stakes inbetween the walls and cover them in wallpaper which takes forever – it’s not hard or complicated, just tedious. We have guides on how to display the merchandise on the shelves but more often than not we don’t have enough to fill the shelves.

…what condition was the store in when you first walked in?

My first day I had to manually open the automatic doors at the front of the store since the power was off. There was no one there, I thought to myself “what the heck is going on?” and then I walked to the back where I found some people unloading a truck.

Have you heard anything about Spirit Halloween’s online fanbase?

What? What are you talking about?

Okay, there are definitely people like that in the store. There was a little girl, about six years old, she walked through the entire store and called out every animatronic by name and said, “Oh my god they have Bubba!” I was like “What? How do you know this?”

What’s your favorite item?

The soul eater one. It’s a grim reaper holding a girl with fog coming out of her mouth. It’s called the “Harvester of Souls.” But it breaks a lot – my manager was showing me how you have to siphon the fog out of it with your mouth when it breaks down.

Do you have a Halloween costume planned?

I always dress up as what my mom wants me to go as. Last year I was Steve Harrington from “Stranger Things,” this year she wants me to be Billy Butcher from “The Boys.”

Honest, has anything in the store scared you?

No. I see everything get built. Before it even gets scary, I’ve pulled back the curtains and now they’re just goofy to me. There is one kind of scary one that I don’t like. It’s an old lady in a dress that makes old lady zombie noises for 2 minutes at a time. I hate it, it’s annoying, and it makes me uncomfortable.
